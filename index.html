About Me

Hi! I am Ruoyu Li (李若愚 Lǐ, RuòYú), an advocate for the dark sky movement and responsible outdoor lighting. Currently, I am a graduate student in urban data science at NYU CUSP (Center for Urban Science + Progress), and a Data Analysis and Social Impact Intern at DarkSky International.

My work spans across continents. I lead the DarkSky's' New York and Beijing chapters, and co-founded the Shenzhen chapter. I am also active in the geospatial community as the founding treasurer of the NYU chapter of ASPRS (American Society for Photogrammetry and Remote Sensing), a GeoAI, Open Tech, and Student Committee member at GISMO (NYC Geospatial Information Systems and Mapping Organization), and a member of the Chinese Society for Environmental Sciences.

Education: University of Arizona (B.S. Math.; B.S. Info. Sci.; Minors: Stats.+DataSci./ComputerSci./Finance; Certificate: Entrep. and New Venture Development), Columbia University (Graduate Certificate in Advanced Business)

Interests: Photography, astronomy, urban hiking, biking, and spending time with my two lovely cats.
